About me

My name is Hugo Laviec, I am a third year Ph.D. student at the University of Brest (France) where I completed all my studies. I am currently working on the development of new optical imaging modalities and their applications to bioimaging in collaborations with biologists of the University of Brest.

Since the first course of biophotonics followed during my Master's degrees, my interest for original and innovative imaging techniques has grown. The rich history of microscopy and its developments, both theoretical and experimental, have become my favorite field of physics. I love to learn about new (or old) microscopy techniques allowing for revealing interesting contrasts on biological samples. Through the guidance of my thesis supervisors and in collaboration with biologists at the University of Brest, I gained a deeper understanding of the need for new techniques and their applications in biology.
